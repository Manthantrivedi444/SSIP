Be Prepared, Stay Safe

A comprehensive resource for disaster preparedness and management

About Us

DMA is a comprehensive web-based application designed to help communities prepare for, respond to, and recover from natural disasters. Our platform provides real-time alerts, resource coordination, and emergency planning tools to ensure safety and resilience in the face of catastrophic events.

Our Mission

At DMA, our mission is to empower communities with the tools and information they need to effectively manage disaster situations. We believe that with proper preparation and coordination, we can significantly reduce the impact of natural disasters on lives and property.

Our application focuses on three key areas:

  • Early warning systems and predictive analytics
  • Resource coordination and management
  • Community education and preparation

Natural Disasters

  • Earthquakes

    Do's:

    • Drop, cover, and hold on
    • Stay indoors until shaking stops
    • Have emergency supplies ready

    Don'ts:

    • Don't run outside during shaking
    • Don't stand in doorways
    • Don't use elevators

  • Floods

    Do's:

    • Move to higher ground
    • Follow evacuation orders
    • Prepare emergency kit

    Don'ts:

    • Don't walk through flowing water
    • Don't drive through flooded areas
    • Don't touch electrical equipment if wet

  • Tsunamis

    Do's:

    • Move inland or to higher ground
    • Follow evacuation routes
    • Listen to emergency broadcasts

    Don'ts:

    • Don't stay in low-lying coastal areas
    • Don't return until authorities allow
    • Don't go to the beach to watch

  • Hurricanes/Cyclones

    Do's:

    • Board up windows
    • Prepare emergency supplies
    • Follow evacuation orders

    Don'ts:

    • Don't ignore evacuation orders
    • Don't go outside during the storm
    • Don't use candles for lighting

  • Tornadoes

    Do's:

    • Seek shelter in basement or interior room
    • Cover your head
    • Listen to weather alerts

    Don'ts:

    • Don't stay in mobile homes
    • Don't try to outrun a tornado in a vehicle
    • Don't stay near windows

  • Wildfires

    Do's:

    • Create defensible space around home
    • Follow evacuation orders immediately
    • Keep emergency supplies ready

    Don'ts:

    • Don't wait to evacuate
    • Don't leave yard debris near structures
    • Don't return until authorities say it's safe

  • Landslides

    Do's:

    • Be alert to signs like cracking trees or walls
    • Evacuate if told to do so
    • Contact local officials about hazards

    Don'ts:

    • Don't build near steep slopes
    • Don't stay in affected areas during heavy rainfall
    • Don't ignore unusual sounds like trees cracking

  • Volcanic Eruptions

    Do's:

    • Evacuate volcanic danger zones
    • Wear long sleeves to protect skin from ash
    • Use masks to filter ash particles

    Don'ts:

    • Don't stay in low-lying areas near the volcano
    • Don't drive in heavy ash fall
    • Don't return until authorities declare it safe

  • Droughts

    Do's:

    • Conserve water
    • Install water-efficient fixtures
    • Follow local water restrictions

    Don'ts:

    • Don't waste water on non-essential activities
    • Don't ignore leaks in plumbing
    • Don't water lawns during restrictions

Man-Made Disasters

  • Industrial Accidents

    Do's:

    • Follow evacuation procedures
    • Report incidents to authorities
    • Stay upwind of chemical releases

    Don'ts:

    • Don't ignore safety warnings
    • Don't return until it's declared safe
    • Don't touch spilled chemicals

  • Chemical Spills

    Do's:

    • Evacuate the area immediately
    • Call emergency services
    • Close windows and turn off ventilation if indoors

    Don'ts:

    • Don't touch or walk through spilled chemicals
    • Don't try to clean up hazardous spills yourself
    • Don't return until authorities say it's safe

  • Nuclear Disasters

    Do's:

    • Take shelter in a basement or central room
    • Follow evacuation orders immediately
    • Take potassium iodide only if instructed

    Don'ts:

    • Don't look at the flash of a nuclear explosion
    • Don't consume food or water that may be contaminated
    • Don't ignore official instructions

  • Oil Spills

    Do's:

    • Report spills to authorities
    • Avoid contact with oil
    • Follow cleanup instructions

    Don'ts:

    • Don't use dispersants without authorization
    • Don't swim in affected water
    • Don't consume seafood from affected areas

  • Fires (Urban & Industrial)

    Do's:

    • Call emergency services immediately
    • Use stairs, not elevators, during evacuation
    • Have fire extinguishers accessible

    Don'ts:

    • Don't try to fight large fires yourself
    • Don't hide during a fire
    • Don't go back into a burning building

  • Transportation Accidents

    Do's:

    • Stay buckled/secure during transportation
    • Be aware of emergency exits
    • Follow crew instructions

    Don'ts:

    • Don't panic during emergencies
    • Don't block aisles with luggage
    • Don't ignore safety briefings

  • Pandemic (COVID-19)

    Do's:

    • Wash hands frequently
    • Wear masks in crowded places
    • Maintain physical distance
    • Get vaccinated

    Don'ts:

    • Don't touch face with unwashed hands
    • Don't attend large gatherings when sick
    • Don't ignore symptoms
    • Don't share personal items
